How compounding works

Compound interest is the addition of interest to the principal sum, so that interest earns interest.

Key Principles:

  • Time: The longer you wait, the faster it grows.
  • Rate: Small changes in yield lead to massive final differences.
  • Consistency: Regular annual additions fuel the engine.
